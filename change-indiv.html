Ready. Set. Change!

Preparing yourself for navigating through change.

Change. Sometimes you're all for it. Other times it "happens" to you whether you like it or not. Every change — personal or professional — requires you to think and act differently than you do during "normal" times.


The good news is, the patterns of every kind of change are consistent. Even if you're in the middle of overwhelming change, you can shift the way you approach things and regain control.


Turner People Development has you covered. Please find the tools and information you might need to help you capture and regain control of your approach and journey through change.

 

Our reactions to change & how we transition

Let's get some definitions out of the way first. Changes are events that are situational and external to us. Transitions are processes — psychological reorientations, if you will.


Individuals transition through disruption or change at their own pace. Many go through these three stages below quickly. Others go through in a more measured manner. This should help you understand your reactions and feelings in relation to the natural process of dealing with change.



Practical Application

Take a change or disruption you're experiencing now, either personally or at work. Where do you see yourself on this chart? How does this information help you better plan for your approaches for managing change? How could you focus your energy that gives you the most "bang" out of your efforts?

3 Strategies to Manage Disruption

There are three basic strategies for dealing with challenges. Each of these strategies is useful in certain situations and you will often use a combination of them.


Strategy What is it Tips & Tactics Practical Application
Reframe the challenge Redefine the situation in a way that reduces or resolves the challenge. Recognize the difference between facts of a situation and the stories you create to explain them.

Question your explanation of the cause. Ask yourself if there are other ways to think about your situation.

Ask yourself how someone you know that is generally optimistic might view the situation — put yourself in their POV for a second.

Shift your view of something from a threat to an opportunity.

 Think of a challenge you are currently facing. Can you reframe it or some aspect of it as an opportunity or look at it in a way that reduces its impact on you?
Change the Situation Do something to change reality. Fix the real problem. Get to the root of the problem.

Select the right tools. Changing a situation depends on the situation and not every "tool" is going to work, which is why you may feel frustrated. Broaden your toolkit.

Use your influence — the key to changing many situations is getting others to shift their thoughts or actions. Rewards, expertise, authority, or character can help. 		Think of a challenge you are currently facing. Are there things you can do to alter the situation and reduce the level of adversity you are experiencing or reduce the duration of the challenge? What tools and forms of influence can you apply?
Accept What It Is Adjust your mental model of the world to include the new reality. Not every situation can be reframed or changed, so you need to spend time on reflecting on how you adapt yourself to the new reality.

Create more accurate expectations. Shift them as well as your thoughts and behaviors to adjust to the new situation as it is.

If you accept the notion of endless change, you are more likely to recognize when it's time to adjust to a new reality instead of holding on to an old pattern.

Set your ego aside.

Connect with others who are experiencing similar changes. 		Think of a challenge you are currently facing. What elements of it are out of your control? What can you do to adjust to a new reality that you may not be happy about?
 
 

Resilience

Resilient people have cultivated seven characteristics to help them solve problems more efficiently, wasting less energy in the process of dealing with challenges. Everyone has these resilience "muscles" and they can be strengthened (or weakened). Regular practice and use will build them up and help you conserve your energy when dealing with change.


How have you used each resilience muscle to overcome a challenge? How balanced do you think or feel your resilience muscles are? Which ones do you tend to underuse? Overuse?
