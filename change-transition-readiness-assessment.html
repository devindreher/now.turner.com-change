Change/Transition Readiness Assessment

Changes in our work environment requires us to transform ourselves — to transform our mindsets, behaviors, fundamental assumptions, ways of relating to others, and how we cause or support the things happening in our organization. This is why we call it a journey. But, do you know where you are today in that journey?

The self assessment below will help you determine where you are — in this moment — during the transitions you're experiencing. Have you said goodbye to the old way of how things are done? Or, are you in the middle — caught between how things done before and how it needs to be done in the future? Are you accepting the new beginnings you are experiencing now that changes are complete and you are in the "future state" of things?

To find out those answers, first read and respond accordingly to these 14 statements. Your responses will guide the assessment to identify where along the path you are today. Click "submit" once you've responded to each statement.

Your results will help guide you through this site and point you to tools that correspond to where you are in your change journey.

This change is important enough to me that I'm OK with dealing with setbacks as they arise.

I am committed to taking part in the change.

I will remain committed to the change in the face of other emerging priorities.

I have clearly identified what is and what isn't ending for me in this transition.

I accept the need to deal with both personal and professional losses/impacts related to this change.

I have a plan to soften the impact on me leaving the past ways of doing things behind.

I have a clear vision of the change.

I feel more confused than upset about this change.

I have created systems that will keep me on track during this confusing time.

I am taking this opportunity to inventory and evaluate my abilities.

I am strengthening those things that are not changing in my life at this time.

I have enough physical and mental stamina to undergo this change.

I have someone to act as a good listener at this time.

I have a plan to manage the stress of this challenging time.
